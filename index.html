Case Study Between


Google Lens

I

and

MobileNet (v2?)


  • Test Image - Water Bottle
    • Output on Google Lens -

    Thermos waterbottle

    Output on MobileNet -

    cocktail shaker


    Result:

    Google Lens is more accurate, but MobileNet is relatively close.


  • Test Image - Handbag
    • Output on Google Lens -

    Primark woven bag

    Output on MobileNet -

    purse


    Result:

    Google Lens is more precise here, but MobileNet is correct this time!


  • Test Image - Pillow
    • Output on Google Lens

    floral pillow cover

    Output on MobileNet -

    pillow


    Result:

    MobileNet is correct here - it identified the object instead of the cover.


  • Test Image - Book
    • Output on Google Lens -

    book

    Output on MobileNet -

    television


    Result:

    Google Lens could see that it was a book from its shape, whilst MobileNet looked at its cover more.


  • Test Image - Glue Stick
    • Output on Google Lens -

    Pritt Stick

    Output on MobileNet -

    hair spray


    Result:

    Google Lens could identify the shape of a glue stick, whilst MobileNet thought it was a hairspray can!


  • Test Image - Pen
    • Output on Google Lens -

    pen

    Output on MobileNet -

    syringe


    Result:

    Google Lens could see that this was a pen, whilst MobileNet looked at its tip and thought it was a syringe!


  • Test Image - Scissors
    • Output on Google Lens -

    scissors

    Output on MobileNet -

    spotlight, spot


    Result:

    Google Lens could identify these scissors correctly, whilst MobileNet acted like they weren't in its training data.


  • Test Image - Fan
    • Output on Google Lens -

    electric fan

    Output on MobileNet -

    electric fan


    Result:

    Whilst both MobileNet and Google Lens identified this electric fan correctly, Google Lens recognised it on the first try!


  • Test Image - Chair
    • Output on Google Lens -

    chair

    Output on MobileNet -

    seatbelt


    Result:

    Google Lens could recognise many different types of chairs, whilst MobileNet was probably only trained to identify one type of chair, and so confused it with a seatbelt!.


  • Test Image - Headphones
    • Output on Google Lens -

    wireless headphones

    Output on MobileNet -

    computer mouse


    Result:

    MobileNet must have been finding it hard to work out what this object actually was, but Google Lens recognised it in a cinch.



    Final Verdict: Google Lens or MobileNet?

    Google Lens is deemed the best image identification technology on the public market, outperforming MobileNet in 9 out of 10 examples. Its issues were probably due to focusing on the wrong thing. However, if the pillow example was repeated with a plain white cover, then the results could have been different.
